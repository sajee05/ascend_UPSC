Q1) Consider the following statements:
1.  The State Information Commission entertains complaints and appeals pertaining to offices, financial institutions and public sector undertakings under the concerned state government.
2.  The State Information Commission is a high-powered independent body.
Which of the statements given above is / are correct?
a)  Only 1
b)  Only 2
c)  Both 1 and 2
d)  Neither 1 nor 2
#QuestionEnd
#AnswerStart
Answer: c)  Both 1 and 2
#AnswerEnd

Q2) The State Information Commission submits its annual report to the:
a)  Governor of the State
b)  High Court of the State
c)  State Legislature
d)  State Government
#QuestionEnd
#AnswerStart
Answer: d)  State Government
#AnswerEnd

Q3) How can a State Information Commissioner be removed from office?
a)  By the order of the State Chief Information Commissioner.
b)  By the order of Supreme Court of India.
c)  By the order of the Governor after the Supreme Court, on a reference made to it by the Governor, has on inquiry reported that the State Information Commissioner ought to be removed.
d)  In a manner similar to the removal of a Judge of a High Court.
#QuestionEnd
#AnswerStart
Answer: c)  By the order of the Governor after the Supreme Court, on a reference made to it by the Governor, has on inquiry reported that the State Information Commissioner ought to be removed.
